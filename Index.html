Air pollution: What, why and how?

The largest environmental risk to public health in the UK, but do you really understand it?

It is estimated that long-term exposure to man-made air pollution in the UK has an annual equivalent to 28,000 to 36,000 deaths.

So, what exactly is air pollution?

Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances in the Earth's atmosphere. These substances can be in the form of solid particles, liquid droplets, or gases, and they can come from both natural and human-made sources. The key issue with air pollution is that it can have adverse effects on the health of living organisms and the overall quality of the environment.

Some common sources of air pollution include:

  • Combustion of Fossil Fuels - eg burning coal & oil
  • Vehicle Emissions - Cars, trucks etc
  • Agricultural Practices - eg fertilizers & pesticides
  • Waste Disposal - Via improper disposal and burning
